About Me

Hey! My name is Morgan. I'm a 2nd year PhD student in the Department of Computational Medicine and Bioinformatics at the University of Michigan. I study how methods from GIS, spatial statistics, and graph theory/network science can be used to quantify spatial relationships in a biological setting. Most of my work involves applying these methods to tumor samples (usually H&E slides or immunohistochemistry stains), but I also look at spatial transcriptomics data in a variety of settings.

I'm actually a Michigan native! I was born in Muskegon, and I received my BS in Math & Computer Science from Grand Valley State University.

One of my favorite parts of science is science communication. I love presenting my work (even though presenting in front of large groups of people is still a bit intimidating!), writing about science, and teaching others.
